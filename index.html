Images are the starting point of many conversations: but once shared, nobody can be sure of where an image can turn up. Our mission is to keep your conversations going, but without giving you back full control of your photos; who sees them, for how long and what can be done with them. All the convenience of online sharing, with the privacy of sharing a printed photo.

With Pixie you will be able to store all your images securely, yet share them with your friends. You can do this without opening them up to anybody other than the intended recipients: even we cannot view them! The app is free and we will only charge premium users (unlimited storage). Our early supporters will be given premium accounts and with higher levels of support they will also be available to give premium accounts to their friends.

FAQ

Why is image privacy not preserved by most current image sharing systems?
Most systems are based on exchanging and storing in the cloud standard JPEG images. These images are not encrypted and anybody who has access to them can view them. In many cases, intended users can save these images and then further share them. Moreover, once posted, images may be searchable by search engines and could be cached by these even after users have removed them.
How about encrypting images? Would that work?
End-to-end encryption, from sender to receiver, would protect the images but it would make it impossible to use existing infrastructure for sharing. Most sharing systems expect to work with JPEG images, and many tools used for sharing, such as resizing images to work at the correct resolution for a given display, would no longer work with encrypted images.
How does Pixie provide privacy?
Pixie's technology removes information from JPEG images and encrypts it. The encrypted image information is shared separately from the JPEG image, and the JPEG image can be processed and resized as commonly done with existing systems.
